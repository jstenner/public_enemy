The Public Enemy technology.

This piece utilizes WebGL, real-time, in-browser, physics simulation and other graphics techniques that tax even newer computing devices. Firefox or Chrome are recommended browsers. With those browsers, it should run fine on most newer laptops and desktops, depending on the capabilities of your graphics card. iOS devices (iPhones, iPads) rely on Safari, which exhibits problems with the physics simulation and runs rather slowly. If your system meets these requirements, and The Public Enemy button is visible below, click to enter the environment.

Otherwise, click the link below to view screenshots of the piece and the twitter feed generated by the work.

Instructions

Click the mouse to hurl a chair! A collection of timely Twitter hashtags will rain down on the DNC/RNC. When a tag collides with either party, a tweet, using that tag as source, will be generatively composed and posted to Twitter.

Sentences generated by The Public Enemy are displayed in the feedback area at the top. You can zoom in and out with a scroll wheel and rotate with click/drag. IF you have a BEEFY computer, pressing "1" will change to DUMP mode. The number of chairs dumped is proportional to the popularity (see number displayed) of the tag selected by clicking a bubble. This can be memory intensive -- you have been warned :-)
